History

On January 15, 1908, 16 young college women started Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc. at Howard University in Washington, DC. Established to encourage high ethical and moral standards, create lifelong friendships, be a network of support for college women of equal talent and strength, and most importantly, to be of service to all mankind. More than a century later, led by the spirit of the founders, we continue on that path. Nine juniors and seniors initiated the group and invited 7 sophomores to join them. Together, they created the mission, colors, symbols of recognitions and purpose that all members hold near and dear to their hearts.

Recognition

Colors

The official colors are Salmon Pink and Apple Green. However, members of the sorority have embraced all combinations of pink and green, from blush to fuchsia and lime to forest.

The official symbols of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc. are the ivy leaf, the pink tea rose and 20 pearls. In January and March 2017, Alpha Kappa Alpha became the first known sorority to trademark their call, "Skee Wee"® (US Reg.5,116,853) and hand sign, "the pinky"®(US Reg.5,153,550) with the US Patent and Trademark Office.

Membership

During its 111 year existence, Alpha Kappa Alpha initiated or extended membership to over 300,000 women. Current active membership is over 75,000 college women or graduates spread across the United States with chapters in 42 out of 50 states and several foreign countries. Divided into 9 regions by geography, active members gather annually at their Regional Conference and biennally at the National Conference to discuss current business and any pending changes to the governing bylaws.

Honorary Membership

As mentioned before, most members of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc. were initiated in college or shortly after college graduation. However, there is a category of honorary membership for trendsetting women who positively impact the lives of their communities in a manner that is consistent with the principles and mission of the sorority. Some of those members include Eleanor Roosevelt, Alicia Keys, Mae Jemison, Nichelle Nichols, Maya Angelou, Ava Duvernay, and Rosa Parks.

Famous (Initiated) Members

Before they were known to the world, they were young women trailblazing their way through college striving to be the best. Women like Sen. Kamala Harris, Katherine Gobel Johnson (Hidden Figures), Phylicia Rashad, Wanda Sykes, and Sonia Sanchez.

Popular Culture

Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc. and its members have been prominently featured in movies and television shows such as "Stomp The Yard", "Different World", any show that features a college or regional step show and unfortunately on VH1's "Sorority Sisters" which was thankfully cancelled very early in its first season.

Step Show Performance

This Is a SERIOUS MATTER!, is the signature performance of Alpha Kappa Alpha members. Known as the "pretty girls", Serious Matter is usually the routine that allows members to rest and take a breath while still stepping and being pretty.

Economic Impact

If they make it in pink and green, the women of Alpha Kappa Alpha will probably buy it which amounts to millions of dollars in revenue. The economic prowess and impact that Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc. is well established. In the corporate world, Mattel®, Coach and Macy's are prime examples of the AKA experience; Mattel® created a commemorative Centennial Barbie for members for a nominal price of $35. 10 years later, they on eBay for over $300 and more. Governments get excited when hosting an AKA conference, economic influxes of $5 million to New Orleans in 2007, to Houston, TX in 2018; the sorority and its members contributed $30 million to the local economy.
